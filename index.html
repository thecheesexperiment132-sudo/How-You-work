Key Neurochemicals

The Synaptic Spark

01

Synthesis & Storage

Chemicals are created from amino acids (like Tryptophan for Serotonin) and stored in tiny sacs called vesicles at the end of neurons.

02

The Release (Exocytosis)

When an electrical pulse hits the end of the nerve, these sacs burst, spilling chemicals into the 'synapse'—the gap between cells.

03

Receptor Binding

The chemical floats across and clicks into a receptor on the next cell like a key in a lock, triggering a new electrical pulse.
